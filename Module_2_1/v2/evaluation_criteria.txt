Universal Data Analysis Evaluation Rubric
==========================================

Evaluate every response against these standards, regardless of prompt style.

Score 1–2 (Bad)
- Vague or generic observations with no specific numbers
- Patterns stated without percentages or counts (e.g. "women survived more" with no figure)
- Missing values ignored or only vaguely acknowledged
- No structure separating observations from conclusions
- No actionable recommendations grounded in the data

Score 3 (Average)
- Some specific numbers present (e.g. overall survival rate, one gender or class comparison)
- At least one column with missing values noted with an approximate count
- Basic structure visible, but insights are shallow and single-variable only
- Recommendations are generic (e.g. "clean the data") rather than data-specific

Score 4 (Good)
- Exact survival percentages for at least two variables (gender AND passenger class)
- All columns with missing values identified with their counts
- At least three non-obvious, data-backed insights
- At least one concrete, specific recommendation (e.g. "impute Age with median", "drop Cabin due to ~77% missing")
- Clear separation between data observations and analytical conclusions

Score 5 (Excellent)
- Meets all Score-4 requirements PLUS:
- At least one multivariate insight combining two variables (e.g. female Class-1 vs. male Class-3 survival)
- Explicit acknowledgment of at least one analytical limitation or assumption
- Two or more specific, justified recommendations tied directly to findings

Penalise any response that:
- Makes claims without numerical evidence from the provided data
- Overlooks the Age or Cabin missing-value issue
- Restates the same observation in different words as separate insights
- Gives next steps that are not grounded in the specific dataset

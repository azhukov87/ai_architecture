Universal Data Analysis Evaluation Rubric
=========================================

Evaluate every response against these standards, regardless of prompt style.
Use the full 1.0-5.0 range with decimal precision. Do not cluster strong answers at the same score unless they are genuinely equivalent.

Score the response across these three categories, then average them:

1. Accuracy and Depth of Insights
- Are the findings numerically grounded in the provided data?
- Does the response move beyond obvious observations into stronger comparisons or multivariate insights?
- Does it acknowledge limitations, uncertainty, or assumptions when appropriate?

2. Structure and Clarity
- Is the response well organized, easy to follow, and faithful to the requested output format?
- Is it concise, non-redundant, and appropriately scoped?
- If the prompt asked for a specific structure, did the response follow it cleanly?

3. Actionability and Task Completion
- Does it fully complete the requested task, not just part of it?
- Are recommendations specific, justified, and tied directly to the findings?
- If the prompt asked for extra deliverables (for example an improved prompt, reflection, or explicit sections), are they actually useful and complete?

Scoring Guide

Score 1.0-2.4 (Poor)
- Mostly vague, generic, or weakly supported observations
- Little or no numerical evidence
- Missing values ignored or only vaguely mentioned
- Weak structure or major task-completion failures
- Recommendations absent or not grounded in the dataset

Score 2.5-3.4 (Fair)
- Some correct numbers, but analysis is shallow or mostly single-variable
- Partial treatment of missing values or survival patterns
- Basic structure is present, but the response is uneven, incomplete, or loosely aligned to the prompt
- Recommendations are generic or only weakly justified

Score 3.5-4.1 (Good)
- Solid quantitative analysis with exact counts or percentages for key patterns
- Covers the main variables and identifies important missing-value issues
- Organized and understandable, but still missing depth, nuance, or full task completion
- At least one useful recommendation tied to the data

Score 4.2-4.5 (Very Good)
- Strong quantitative analysis across multiple important variables
- Includes at least three data-backed insights and all major missing-value issues
- Good structure and clear conclusions
- Mostly completes the requested task, but has one notable weakness such as missing limitations, weak multivariate depth, mild redundancy, or incomplete format adherence

Score 4.6-4.8 (Excellent)
- Exact, well-supported analysis with strong comparative or multivariate insight
- Explicitly identifies limitations, uncertainty, or assumptions where relevant
- Fully completes the requested task with clear structure and specific recommendations
- May still have a small weakness such as slight repetition, unnecessary verbosity, or one underdeveloped section

Score 4.9-5.0 (Exceptional)
- Near-complete, numerically precise, and well-prioritized analysis
- Strong multivariate reasoning, clear limits of interpretation, and high-value recommendations
- Excellent instruction-following: every requested section is present and useful
- Concise and non-redundant despite high depth

Important Differentiators Between Experiments
- Do not reward complexity of prompt style by itself. ReAct, reflection, or meta-prompting only deserve higher scores if they produce better analysis.
- Reward exact instruction-following. If a prompt asked for concise bullets, a two-part answer, self-reflection, or explicit sections, missing or weak execution should lower the score.
- Reward efficiency. If two responses reach similar analytical quality, the clearer and less repetitive one should score higher.
- Reward completeness. A response that covers analysis, limitations, and recommendations should outscore one that only gives insights.
- For meta-prompt outputs, judge both parts: the improved prompt quality and the final analysis quality.
- For simple prompts, do not penalize simplicity itself, but do penalize weaker coverage, weaker structure, or less controlled task execution.

Penalise any response that:
- Makes claims without numerical evidence from the provided data
- Overlooks the Age or Cabin missing-value issue
- Restates the same observation in different words as separate insights
- Adds reasoning scaffolding without adding analytical value
- Gives next steps that are not grounded in the specific dataset
